Common name:

Kaanjiram

Plant name:

Poison Nut Tree

Scientific name:

Strychnos nux-vomica

Ayurvedic properties:

Digestive Aid, Stimulant, Anti-inflammatory, Nervine, Therapeutic Uses, Toxicity

Description:

The Poison Nut Tree is known for its highly toxic seeds, which contain strychnine, a potent alkaloid. While the tree is native to tropical Asia and Australia, it also has traditional medicinal uses in small, controlled doses. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and features glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. Despite its toxicity, various parts of the tree are utilized in traditional medicine for different ailments. Care must be taken around this tree due to the danger posed by its seeds, making it essential to understand its uses and risks.
